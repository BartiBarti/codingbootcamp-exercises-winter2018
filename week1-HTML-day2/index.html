Lodz

Industrial City in the center of Poland

Łódź (/wuːtʃ/ WOOTCH, /lɒdz/ LODZ;[1] Polish: [wutɕ] (About this sound listen); Yiddish: לאדזש‎, Lodzh; also written as Lodz) [2] is the third-largest city in Poland and a former industrial centre. Link to Lodz website Located in the central part of the country, it has a population of 693,797 (2017). [3] It is the capital of Łódź Voivodeship, and is approximately 135 kilometres (84 mi) south-west of Warsaw. The city's coat of arms is an example of canting: depicting a boat. It alludes to the city's name which translates literally as "boat."
